Tokyo Runner: Prologue

The flat wakes me. Not the light, the flat itself. The walls press in, humming that low corporate frequency. I am Runner_303. The name is a coat I put on.

A message buzzes in my teeth. Ghost. A job. A way out. The specifics are a shimmer, but the weight of it is real. My account balance is a shrug. Rent is a fact.

I go to the alley. The rain here isn't water, it's the city sweating out last night's synthetic alcohol. Ghost is there—a person-shaped gap in the air. They talk about Nexus Corp, about a sister taken. The chip is warm in my hand. I put it in my pocket.

I say I'm in. The plan is a map drawn on fog. We choose the front door. The bold choice. The stupid choice. I walk into the lobby, a fiction walking through fact. I hack the system, become Dr. Chen, a lie so confident the truth steps aside.

The server room is silent—a quiet that has swallowed screams. I jack in. Data isn't ones and zeroes. It's memory, feeling, the cold fear of a woman on a steel table. Ghost's sister, being smoothed into something dull and usable.

Then the alarm. I run. I burst out into neon night, sending the data like a whisper. Ghost replies: Thank you. The story has changed. I'm a room where a terrible, beautiful secret now lives. And it's looking out through my eyes. The end is just the beginning telling itself a different story.
